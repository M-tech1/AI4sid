## 🩸 BloodTreat AI

### Intelligent Blood Supply Management System for Nigeria

📌 Overview

BloodTreat AI is an AI-powered blood forecasting and distribution platform designed to reduce blood shortages across Nigeria.

The system leverages machine learning, predictive analytics, and inventory optimization to improve coordination between hospitals, enhance donor mobilization, and prevent blood wastage.

Nigeria currently faces recurring blood shortages due to poor demand forecasting, inefficient logistics, and lack of real-time visibility into regional blood inventory levels. BloodTreat AI addresses these challenges using data-driven intelligence.

### 🚨 Problem

Nigeria requires approximately 1.8–2 million units of blood annually, yet supply consistently falls short.

Current challenges include:

Emergency blood shortages

Expired unused blood units

Poor donor engagement planning

Weak inter-hospital coordination

Limited visibility into inventory levels

Blood has a short shelf life (35–42 days), making accurate demand prediction critical.

### 💡 Proposed Solution

BloodTreat AI consists of four core components:

1️⃣ Demand Prediction Model

Uses historical hospital data

Analyzes seasonal and public health trends

Predicts blood demand by hospital, region, and blood type

Built using supervised learning and time-series forecasting models (LSTM, ARIMA, Random Forest)

2️⃣ Inventory Optimization Engine

Monitors real-time blood inventory

Flags near-expiry units

Suggests redistribution between hospitals

Recommends donation drives based on forecasted shortages

3️⃣ Smart Donor Targeting System

Identifies high-demand blood types

Predicts donor response likelihood

Sends targeted SMS/app notifications

Uses classification models (Logistic Regression, XGBoost)

4️⃣ Dashboard & Decision Support System

Provides:

Real-time availability map

Shortage risk alerts

Demand forecast graphs

Expiry notifications

📊 Required Data

Historical blood usage (2–5 years)

Blood type distribution data

Donor records (anonymized)

Expiry timelines

Seasonal admission records

Accident & emergency reports

Maternal health data

Potential data sources:

Federal Ministry of Health

National Blood Service Commission

Teaching hospitals

Red Cross Nigeria

### 🧠 Learning Approach

Primary: Supervised Learning (Regression & Time-Series Forecasting)
Secondary: Reinforcement Learning (for redistribution optimization)

🎯 Success Metrics

40% reduction in emergency shortages

30% reduction in expired blood wastage

Forecast accuracy above 85% (MAPE)

Emergency response time reduced to <15 minutes

25% increase in donor engagement rate
